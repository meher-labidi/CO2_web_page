CO₂ (R744) Refrigeration Systems

Two main configurations: Secondary Cooling & Transcritical Systems

Secondary Cooling Systems

CO₂ as chilled fluid, no phase change

CO₂ circulates as a chilled fluid and absorbs heat without changing state. Ideal for applications where simplicity and safety are paramount.

  • Simpler design with reduced complexity
  • Lower refrigerant charge
  • Enhanced safety
Perfect for: Data centers, industrial process cooling

Transcritical CO₂ Systems

High efficiency with heat recovery

Today's advanced system designs minimize reliance on HFCs. CO₂ operates as a direct-expansion refrigerant, transferring cooling efficiently.

  • Operates efficiently at low and high temperatures
  • Simultaneous heating & cooling – reclaim heat for other processes
  • Up to 20% energy savings vs traditional HFCs (even in warm climates)
Best For: Supermarkets, breweries, dairy (heat spaces while cooling freezers, recover heat for brewing, chill milk and produce hot water)

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about CO₂ and R290 refrigeration systems

Why choose natural refrigerants over traditional refrigerants?

Natural refrigerants like CO₂ (R744) and R290 (Propane) have minimal environmental impact with GWP of 1 and 3 respectively, compared to thousands for traditional HFCs. CO₂ is non-flammable (A1) ideal for large centralized systems, while R290 offers excellent energy efficiency for self-contained units. Both comply with current and future environmental regulations.

What's the difference between CO₂ and R290 systems?

CO₂ (R744) is used primarily in large centralized systems like supermarket racks, cold storage, and industrial applications. It operates at very high pressures and is non-flammable. R290 (Propane) is used in self-contained units like merchandisers, display cases, and smaller refrigeration equipment. It offers excellent energy efficiency but is flammable, so systems use low refrigerant charges (<150g) for safety.

What kind of energy savings can I expect?

Danfoss CO₂ systems can achieve up to 20% energy savings compared to traditional HFC systems, even in warm climates, and up to 30% reduction in carbon footprint at store level. R290 single-circuit systems can offer significant energy savings as well.
